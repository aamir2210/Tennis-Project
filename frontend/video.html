Here is a Step by Step Guide to Perfect Your Serve:

  1. Grip It Right: Embrace the continental grip from the get-go. It's your foundation for a killer serve.
  2. Step-by-Step Learning: Coordinate your serve with progressions. Take it step by step, and you'll find your rhythm.
  3. Perfect Your Toss: Treat your ball toss seriously. Aim for a 1 PM time on the clock – not too high, not too low.
  4. Toss Troubles? Retry: If your toss goes south, don't hit it. Toss it again. Legally, you have as many chances as you need.
  5. Get your back leg behind the ball: Getting your back leg and weight behind the ball allows for easier weight transfer and a more consistent shot.
  6. Location is Key: Your serve should be over your head, not just off your shoulder.
  7. Finish Strong: Wrap up your serve over the baseline, then smoothly move back into position behind the baseline.
  8. Avoid Foot Faults: Contact with the toss past the baseline results in a foot fault. Stay behind the baseline, and you're in the clear.

Here Are Some Tips and Tricks to Follow to Ensure the Perfect Shots:

  1. Early preparation: You can never prepare yourself soon enough. Turn your hips and shoulders before the oncoming ball bounces on your side.
  2. Exaggerate your follow through: Follow all the way through your shot after your hit. Hit the ball, follow through, and move your feet.
  3. Hit-recover: Instead of focusing on your shot after you’ve made it, focus on the recover. Hit, finish and get back into position.
  4. Keep your head still: If you find yourself mis-hitting shots, be sure that you aren’t moving your head as you swing. Follow the ball onto the racquet and keep your head still until the shot has been played.
  5. Get your back leg behind the ball: Getting your back leg and weight behind the ball allows for easier weight transfer and a more consistent shot.
